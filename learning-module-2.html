Consonants

  • Memorize these symbols, this is the starting point.
  • These symbols is just a consonant but a consonant only!
  • Do not forget to put that plus (+) sign at the bottom of each symbol because that
    indicates that the letter is just a consonant or a single letter.

vowels

  • a is just an a
  • e and i in Filipino alphabet, sounds alike, that’s why they are represented by the same symbol.
  • o and u, also sounds alike that’s why they are represented by the same symbol.

remember that...

  • This is a different case, writing A, E/I, O/U as vowels is different when they are written together with a consonant

A's

This is the 1st set of Basic Consonants, the same consonants in step 1 but with vowel “A“

  • All you have to do is, remove the plus (+) sign under the symbols. This already indicates that the consonant is with a letter “a“

E's or I's

This is the 2nd set of consonants with the vowel “e/i“

In Filipino Alphabet, the vowel “E” and “I” are almost the same in sound. That’s why they are represented by the same particular symbol.

  • In this case, a consonant is paired with a vowel “e/i“. (ex. rin and din)
  • Put a line (-), a wavy line (), or even a dot () above each symbols.
  • That symbol above means that the consonant is with a vowel “e or i” only!

O's or U's

In Filipino Alphabet, the vowel “O” and “U” are almost the same in sound. That’s why they are represented by the same particular symbol.

  • In this case, a consonant is paired with a vowel “o/u“.
  • Put a line (-), a wavy line (), or even a dot () below each symbols.
  • That symbol above means that the consonant is with a vowel “o or u” only!

Period and Comma

A sentence will not be completed without the stop and the pause symbols.

  • “II” and “I” in Baybayin is the same as the “.” and “,” in the English alphabet.
  • “II” is for the period (.)
  • “I” is for the comma (,)
